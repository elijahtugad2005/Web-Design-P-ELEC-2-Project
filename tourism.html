History of
Medellin

The name Medellin originates from a village in the Badajoz province of Extremadura, Spain. It became an official municipality on September 9, 1881, throuh a royal decree issued by Queen Isabel II of Spain.

Initially, Medellin was part of Daanbantayan and began as a town with only three barangays: Kawit and Buenavista on the west coast,and Tindog on the east coast. Kawit, being the largest and most developed barangay at the first seat of government. However, due to its deep waters, the area became vulnerable to pirate attacks, prompting the government to move to Buenavista (Daanlungsod). Unfortunately, Buenavista's shallow waters made it difficult for traders to transport goods. South of Buenavista was a place called Tawagan, which was more suitable for trade and commerce. When the government seat relocated to Tawagan, the new town center was named Medellin.
